# Letters, Lore & Strategy

> *The life of the mind and the collector's cabinet.*

| # | Badge | Achievement |
|---|-------|-------------|
| 1 | The Deep Reader | Read a monumental text in its entirety — *The Art of Computer Programming* (Knuth), *Critique of Pure Reason* (Kant), *Ulysses* (Joyce), or a significant portion of the Great Books series — and explain its core thesis to a layperson. |
| 2 | The Dungeon Master | Write and run a tabletop RPG campaign as Game Master for at least eight regular sessions — a complete story arc from hook to resolution. |
| 3 | The Grand Strategist | Learn and play at least two full games of high-complexity grand strategy board games — from different systems such as Twilight Imperium, Advanced Squad Leader, War of the Ring, or equivalent — and demonstrate chess proficiency by studying openings, endgames, and tactics well enough to win a rated game or solve a set of composed problems. |
| 4 | The Philologist | Achieve functional competency in a constructed language (Esperanto, Lojban, Quenya, or Toki Pona) — enough to translate a poem or short passage. |
| 5 | The Knights Who Say Ni | Memorize a scene from *Monty Python and the Holy Grail* and perform it in costume, with appropriate voices and props — to a live audience who can verify accuracy against the script. |
| 6 | The Speedrunner | Complete a speedrun of a video game and submit it to a public leaderboard — demonstrating mastery of routing, glitch exploitation, and frame-perfect execution. |
| 7 | The Martial Artist | Train in a Historical European Martial Art (longsword, rapier, or saber) and demonstrate proficiency by executing a scored bout under HEMA tournament rules, and achieve consistent groupings on a target at 20 yards with a traditional bow (recurve or longbow). |
| 8 | The Collector | Build a curated collection — historically significant coins, scientific instruments, natural specimens, or a full cabinet of curiosities — correctly identified, catalogued, and with documented provenance. |
| 9 | The Homesteader | Grow or raise the principal ingredients for a complete meal and prepare it from scratch — garden to table — demonstrating cultivation, harvest, and cookery. |
| **FINAL BOSS** | **The Treatise** | **Write, illustrate, typeset, and publish an original work — pamphlet, monograph, or field guide — on a subject you have mastered through this curriculum, and present it to a society, meetup, or community of practitioners.** |
