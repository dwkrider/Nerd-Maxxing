# Mathematics & Cryptography

> *The language underneath reality — made tangible and visible.*

| # | Badge | Achievement |
|---|-------|-------------|
| 1 | The Fractal Mapper | Write a program that renders the Mandelbrot Set with real-time zoom and color mapping, and explore the corresponding Julia sets — navigating and annotating at least three morphologically distinct regions along the boundary. |
| 2 | The Cipher Breaker | Crack a monoalphabetic substitution cipher by hand using frequency analysis, then implement a polyalphabetic cipher and break one using Kasiski examination. |
| 3 | The Attractor | Graph a chaotic strange attractor (Lorenz, Rossler, or Chen) as an interactive 3D visualization with adjustable parameters, and map the system's bifurcation diagram showing the transitions between periodic and chaotic behavior. |
| 4 | The Fourier Analyst | Decompose a complex sound wave into its constituent sine waves using an FFT, reconstruct the original signal from the components, and synthesize a recognizable new sound by manually combining pure harmonics. |
| 5 | The Penrose Tiler | Produce a physical aperiodic tiling — Penrose rhombs or the "Hat" monotile — covering at least one square meter. |
| 6 | The Tensegrity Builder | Construct a physical tensegrity structure of at least 12 struts that supports meaningful weight through tension alone, and document the force equilibrium analysis. |
| 7 | The Origamist | Fold a mathematically precise modular polyhedron from at least 30 individual units (Sonobe icosahedron or equivalent) and explain the underlying geometric symmetry group. |
| 8 | The Proof Writer | Write a formal mathematical proof of a non-trivial theorem and present it to a peer who can verify its correctness. |
| 9 | The Topologist | Build a physical model of a non-orientable surface (Klein bottle or Mobius mechanism) and a 4D projection (tesseract or Schlegel diagram), and prove the surface's non-orientability using its topological properties. |
| **FINAL BOSS** | **The Generative Artisan** | **Write a custom mathematical engine that directly drives a hardware output device — pen plotter, laser cutter, or CNC — to produce a series of at least 10 unique physical generative artworks where no two outputs are identical, with a written explanation of the mathematical system driving the variation.** |
