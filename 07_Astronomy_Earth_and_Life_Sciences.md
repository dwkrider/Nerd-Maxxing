# Astronomy, Earth & Life Sciences

> *Field science and the natural world — observation, collection, and classification.*

| # | Badge | Achievement |
|---|-------|-------------|
| 1 | The Galileo | Align a telescope and observe the Galilean moons of Jupiter or the rings of Saturn with your own eyes. |
| 2 | The Deep Sky | Capture a long-exposure astrophotograph of a nebula, galaxy, or star cluster through a tracking mount. |
| 3 | The Herbalist | Collect, press, dry, and mount a herbarium of at least 25 local plant species with correct Linnaean binomial nomenclature. |
| 4 | The Geologist | Identify 20 minerals or rock types in the field using hardness tests, streak plates, and crystal structure, and locate, extract, and correctly identify a fossil specimen in its geological context. |
| 5 | The Mycologist | Forage and correctly identify at least 10 wild mushroom species using spore prints, morphology, and habitat analysis. |
| 6 | The Stratospheric | Launch a high-altitude weather balloon with a payload that captures video and GPS telemetry before returning to Earth. |
| 7 | The Field Biologist | Conduct a biodiversity survey of a terrestrial or aquatic ecosystem, collecting, identifying, and classifying at least 15 species by taxonomic order and family. |
| 8 | The Beekeeper | Establish and maintain a colony of bees — or breed and rear an exotic invertebrate species through a full life cycle — demonstrating knowledge of husbandry, habitat, and biology. |
| 9 | The Aquascaper | Design and establish a planted aquarium or vivarium as a self-sustaining ecosystem, with deliberate hardscape composition and thriving biological inhabitants. |
| **FINAL BOSS** | **The Field Station** | **Establish a long-term field observation site — document the local geology, catalogue the flora and fauna, track seasonal changes, and produce a comprehensive natural history of the site with astrophotographic context.** |
