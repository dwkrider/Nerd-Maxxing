# Nerd Maxxing: The Century of Badges

> *These are not badges for consuming nerd culture. They are badges for creation and mastery. To complete this list is to become a true polymath — a master of the digital, the physical, the theoretical, and the historical.*

---

## The 10 Categories

Each category contains **9 achievements and 1 Final Boss**. The Final Boss is a capstone that synthesizes the skills of its entire category. Complete all 10 Final Bosses to attempt **The Omni-Boss**.

---

### 1. [Fabrication & Materials Engineering](01_Fabrication_and_Materials_Engineering.md)

The art of turning raw material into precision objects. This category spans the full range of physical making — subtractive manufacturing (CNC milling, lathe turning), additive manufacturing (3D printing), casting and mold-making, laser cutting, and structural engineering. You must command metal, polymer, and wood with equal confidence and understand the material science behind each.

---

### 2. [Electronics, RF & Signals](02_Electronics_RF_and_Signals.md)

Commanding the flow of electrons and the invisible spectrum. From soldering your first joint to designing a custom PCB, this category covers analog and digital circuits, microcontrollers, RF engineering, software-defined radio, and the resurrection of vintage hardware. You must be as comfortable with an oscilloscope as you are with a soldering iron.

---

### 3. [Computing, Systems & AI](03_Computing_Systems_and_AI.md)

Mastery over the entire digital stack — from bare-metal operating systems to machine intelligence. This category covers kernel compilation, networking, databases, cryptographic implementation, reverse engineering, language design, self-hosting, and machine learning. The emphasis is on timeless fundamentals: understanding systems deeply enough to build, secure, and automate them on infrastructure you own.

---

### 4. [Vehicles, Flight & Robotics](04_Vehicles_Flight_and_Robotics.md)

Everything that moves under its own power. This category covers internal combustion engines, electric vehicles, drones, RC aircraft, model rocketry, autonomous robots, marine craft, bicycle mechanics, and kinematic linkages. You must understand propulsion, control systems, aerodynamics, and the mechanical sympathy required to keep machines alive.

---

### 5. [Mathematics & Cryptography](05_Mathematics_and_Cryptography.md)

The language underneath reality. This category bridges pure mathematics and its physical manifestations — fractals, chaos theory, Fourier analysis, cryptographic systems, game theory, topology, generative algorithms, and mathematical origami. The goal is not just to compute, but to make the invisible structures of math tangible and visible.

---

### 6. [Physics & Chemistry](06_Physics_and_Chemistry.md)

The Natural Philosopher's laboratory. This category demands hands-on encounters with fundamental forces at a serious level — building cloud chambers, producing holograms, constructing lasers from discrete components, demonstrating superconductivity, achieving vacuum and plasma, and performing multi-step chemical synthesis. The Final Boss is building a Farnsworth fusor and detecting neutrons from nuclear fusion.

---

### 7. [Astronomy, Earth & Life Sciences](07_Astronomy_Earth_and_Life_Sciences.md)

Field science and the natural world. Where Physics & Chemistry happens at the lab bench, this category happens under open sky and in the field. It covers telescopic observation, astrophotography, geology and fossil hunting, botanical collection and taxonomy, ecology, mycology, and atmospheric science. The Victorian naturalist-collector lives here.

---

### 8. [The Analog Arts](08_The_Analog_Arts.md)

Heritage craftsmanship in the digital age. This category celebrates the physical, tactile, and irreplaceable arts that no algorithm can replicate — darkroom photography, letterpress printing, bookbinding, calligraphy, cartography, horology, miniature painting, and the construction of musical instruments and modular synthesizers.

---

### 9. [Letters, Lore & Strategy](09_Letters_Lore_and_Strategy.md)

The life of the mind and the collector's cabinet. This category covers monumental literature, constructed languages, tabletop RPGs, grand strategy wargaming, fermentation science, celestial navigation, historical martial arts, numismatics, and the esoteric humanities that separate a technician from a polymath.

---

### 10. [The Grand Pilgrimage](10_The_Grand_Pilgrimage.md)

One sacred site for each of the other nine categories, spanning all seven continents — the Iron Bridge (UK) for Fabrication, the Parkes Radio Telescope (Australia) for Electronics, UCLA Room 3420 (USA) for Computing, the Santos-Dumont Museum (Brazil) for Flight, Ramanujan's home (India) for Mathematics, the Cavendish Laboratory (UK) for Physics, the Cradle of Humankind (South Africa) for Life Sciences, the Plantin-Moretus Museum (Belgium) for the Analog Arts, and Shackleton's Hut (Antarctica) for Letters and Lore. The Final Boss is recursive: visit the Escher Museum and create a self-referential account of the journey — a strange loop worthy of Hofstadter.

---

## The Omni-Boss: The Prime Architect

> *To claim the title of the ultimate polymath, you must synthesize all 10 disciplines into a single magnum opus.*

**Design, fabricate, and deploy an autonomous electro-mechanical system.** It must:

- Be **machined or fabricated** from raw stock you designed yourself *(Fabrication & Materials)*
- Run on a **custom PCB** of your own design *(Electronics, RF & Signals)*
- Execute **self-hosted software and local AI** on infrastructure you built *(Computing, Systems & AI)*
- Contain **moving mechanical components** with active control systems *(Vehicles, Flight & Robotics)*
- Employ **mathematical algorithms** for signal processing or generative output *(Mathematics & Cryptography)*
- Interact with a **physical phenomenon** — optical, chemical, or electromagnetic *(Physics & Chemistry)*
- Collect **field data** from the natural world *(Astronomy, Earth & Life Sciences)*
- Be **documented** in a hand-bound volume with original illustrations *(The Analog Arts)*
- Demonstrate **cross-domain synthesis** informed by deep reading and historical knowledge *(Letters, Lore & Strategy)*
- Be **installed or demonstrated** at a site of personal or historical significance *(The Grand Pilgrimage)*

*This is the syllabus. Begin.*
