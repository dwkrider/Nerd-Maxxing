# Electronics, RF & Signals

> *Commanding the flow of electrons and the invisible spectrum.*

| # | Badge | Achievement |
|---|-------|-------------|
| 1 | The Surgeon | Diagnose a fault on a circuit board by reading the schematic and tracing signal flow, then desolder and replace the blown surface-mount component, restoring it to function. |
| 2 | The Waverider | Build an analog circuit, then use an oscilloscope to capture, measure, and interpret its signals — verifying that the circuit behaves as designed. |
| 3 | The Crystal Receiver | Build a functional AM crystal radio entirely from scratch — hand-wound coil, germanium diode or cat's whisker detector, and a proper antenna — that receives multiple broadcast stations without external power. |
| 4 | The Antenna Smith | Design and build a directional antenna (Yagi-Uda or helical) for a specific frequency and measure its gain improvement. |
| 5 | The SDR Explorer | Use a Software Defined Radio to receive and decode at least three distinct types of real-world signals — such as ADS-B aircraft transponders, NOAA weather satellite imagery, amateur radio digital modes, or pager traffic — and document the modulation scheme of each. |
| 6 | The Ham | Earn an amateur radio license and make a verified contact on HF, VHF, or UHF bands. |
| 7 | The Resurrector | Take a piece of dead vintage computing hardware (pre-2000), diagnose the fault to component level, and bring it back to life. |
| 8 | The Analog Alchemist | Build a voltage-controlled oscillator from discrete transistors and op-amps that produces a clean sine wave. |
| 9 | The Firmware Flasher | Write and flash custom firmware onto a microcontroller (ESP32, STM32, or AVR) that reads a sensor, serves a live dashboard over a network, and logs data persistently. |
| **FINAL BOSS** | **The Silicon Architect** | **Design an original circuit from schematic, route a custom multi-layer PCB, order the boards, and hand-solder all surface-mount components into a fully working device.** |
