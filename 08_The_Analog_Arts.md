# The Analog Arts

> *Heritage craftsmanship in the digital age — the physical, the tactile, the irreplaceable.*

| # | Badge | Achievement |
|---|-------|-------------|
| 1 | The Darkroom | Shoot, develop, and print a photograph entirely in a wet darkroom — from exposed film to finished silver gelatin print. |
| 2 | The Bookbinder | Sew and bind a hardcover book by hand using Coptic stitch or traditional case binding. |
| 3 | The Cartographer | Draw a detailed, accurate map of a local area by hand using survey measurements and a consistent scale and legend. |
| 4 | The Letterpress | Set movable type (metal or wood) and pull a clean impression on a letterpress or proof press. |
| 5 | The Calligrapher | Master a formal script (Italic, Copperplate, or Blackletter) well enough to letter a complete broadside or poem. |
| 6 | The Horologist | Disassemble, clean, lubricate, and reassemble a mechanical watch or clock movement and regulate it to within ±10 seconds per day. |
| 7 | The Miniaturist | Paint a set of tabletop gaming miniatures to "tabletop standard" — clean base coats, washes, highlights, and based. |
| 8 | The Luthier | Build a functional musical instrument from scratch — cigar box guitar, kalimba, or a theremin from components. |
| 9 | The Patch Cable Wizard | Build a functioning voice on a modular synthesizer (hardware or VCV Rack) using generative sequencing where the music plays itself. |
| **FINAL BOSS** | **The Renaissance Printer** | **Design a typeface or lettering system, set it in movable type or carve it into a block, print an edition of an original text, and bind the pages into a finished volume — from font to book, entirely by your hand.** |
