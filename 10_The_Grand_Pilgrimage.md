# The Grand Pilgrimage

> *Sacred sites where the modern world was forged — one for each discipline, spanning all seven continents.*

| # | Badge | Achievement | Category | Continent |
|---|-------|-------------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | The Iron Bridge | Visit **The Iron Bridge** at Coalbrookdale (Shropshire, UK) — the first major structure ever built from cast iron, and the crucible of the Industrial Revolution. | *Fabrication & Materials* | Europe |
| 2 | The Dish | Visit the **Parkes Radio Telescope** (New South Wales, Australia) — the 64-metre dish that received the Apollo 11 moonwalk broadcast, tracked Voyager, and continues to map the radio sky. | *Electronics, RF & Signals* | Oceania |
| 3 | The First Message | Stand in **Boelter Hall, Room 3420** at UCLA (Los Angeles, CA) — the nondescript room where the first ARPANET message was sent on October 29, 1969. They tried to type "LOGIN" — the system crashed after "LO." | *Computing, Systems & AI* | North America |
| 4 | The Dark Sky | Visit the **Paranal Observatory** in the Atacama Desert (Chile) — home of the Very Large Telescope, perched at 2,635 metres in the driest desert on Earth, where the atmosphere is so clear that astronomers can image exoplanets directly. | *Astronomy, Earth & Life Sciences* | South America |
| 5 | The Prodigy's Home | Visit **Srinivasa Ramanujan's house** in Kumbakonam, Tamil Nadu, India — where the self-taught genius who sent unsolicited letters to G.H. Hardy filled notebooks with theorems that mathematicians are still proving a century later. | *Mathematics & Cryptography* | Asia |
| 6 | The Cavendish | Visit the **Cavendish Laboratory** (Cambridge, UK) — where Thomson discovered the electron, Chadwick identified the neutron, Cockcroft and Walton first split the atom, and Watson and Crick deduced the structure of DNA. | *Physics & Chemistry* | Europe |
| 7 | The Cradle | Trek to the **Cradle of Humankind** at Sterkfontein Caves (Gauteng, South Africa) — where fossils of *Australopithecus africanus* were discovered, pushing the origin of the human lineage back millions of years. The deepest lore there is. | *Letters, Lore & Strategy* | Africa |
| 8 | The Printing House | Visit the **Plantin-Moretus Museum** (Antwerp, Belgium) — a Renaissance printing workshop with the world's oldest surviving printing presses still in their original room, and the only museum on Earth designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site. | *The Analog Arts* | Europe |
| 9 | The Endurance | Visit **Shackleton's Hut** at Cape Royds, Ross Island, Antarctica — base of the Nimrod expedition, preserved with its original provisions. Shackleton's crew later sailed 800 miles across the Southern Ocean in a 22-foot lifeboat — perhaps the greatest feat of small-craft seamanship in history. | *Vehicles, Flight & Robotics* | Antarctica |
| **FINAL BOSS** | **The Strange Loop** | **Visit the Escher Museum (The Hague, Netherlands) — the shrine of visual recursion and impossible geometry. Then create a self-referential account of your complete pilgrimage in which the documentation of the journey becomes part of the journey itself — a strange loop worthy of Hofstadter.** | *This list* | Europe |
